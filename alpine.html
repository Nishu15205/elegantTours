Alpine Lake Trek Kashmir

Overview

Alpine Lake Trek Kashmir is a haven of immense beauty of nature which takes shape in the form of towering mountains, sparkling blue lake and picturesque landscape. The state has many adventure treks. But among all the treks, the Kashmir Alpine Lakes trek is one of the most prominent treks among the adventure junkies. The beauty of the Alpine Lake trek Kashmir is not limited to the beautiful lakes, but it will also take you through the spectacular landscapes of Sonamarg and Nichnai Pass which are surrounded by snow-capped mountains.

Itinerary

  • Day 1: Srinagar (1585 m) - Naranag - 2270.76 m
  • On arrival at Srinagar, meet and drive to the Naranag district.
  • Naranag is a famous basecamp for trekkers to reach Mount Harmukh, Gandarbal, and Satsar lakes.
  • Located 50 km away from the main city of Srinagar.
  • Reach Naranag by sunset and set your camp near the Wangath River.
  • Overnight in the camp.
  • Day 2: Naranag - Gangabal - 3505.2 m
  • After breakfast, start the journey towards Gangabal, situated at the foot of Mount Harmukh.
  • This place is famous for the twin alpine lakes, Gangabal and Nandkol, located at a height of 11,500 ft.
  • The trek includes steep trails and pine forests, crossing Butsheri and the meadows of Bodpathri to reach Trunkhol meadow.
  • After a 6-hour journey, witness the beautiful sight of Gangabal and Nandkol Lake, with snow-covered mountains in the background.
  • Set your camp near Gangabal Lake.
  • Overnight at the camp.
  • Day 3: Gangabal - Satsar - 3688 m
  • After breakfast, start walking towards Satsar Lake, situated at a height of 12,000 ft.
  • Satsar Lake consists of seven small alpine lakes resting in the mountains.
  • Pass through the meadow of Rasbal, where a flowing river mysteriously goes underground and disappears.
  • Explore the beauty of the crystal-clear lakes.
  • Overnight at the camp.
  • Day 4: Satsar - Gadsar - 3718 m
  • After breakfast, begin the trek towards Gadsar Lake.
  • Gadsar Lake, resting at 12,500 ft, is one of the highest alpine lakes in Kashmir.
  • Surrounded by beautiful alpine flowers, the lake stretches up to 0.85 km in the valley.
  • Cross Megandoab and trek towards Kustor Kout, where you will set your camp for the night.
  • After covering 12 km, witness the picturesque view of Gadsar Lake and the valley covered in flowers.
  • Overnight at the camp.
  • Day 5: Gadsar - Krishnasar - Vishnasar - 3660 m
  • After breakfast, start walking from Kustor Kout towards Krishnasar Lake, situated at 12,500 ft.
  • Krishnasar Lake is the sibling lake of Vishnasar Lake.
  • Enjoy an aerial view of both lakes from Gadsar Pass, which stands at 13,800 ft.
  • After trekking for 10 km, reach the beautiful Vishnasar Lake in Vishnasar Valley.
  • The alpine lake rests at an altitude of 3710 m, reflecting the Krishnasar peak on its crystal-clear water.
  • Overnight at the camp.
  • Day 6: Vishnasar - Nichnai - 3608 m
  • After breakfast, depart from Vishnasar Valley and head towards Nichnai Pass at 11,500 ft.
  • Pass through meadows with grazing sheep, indicating the approach to Nichnai Meadow.
  • Upon reaching Nichnai Valley, the trails descend.
  • Set your camp near the plain land across the river valley.
  • Overnight at the camp.
  • Day 7: Nichnai - Sonamarg - 3620 m
  • After breakfast, trek smoothly down Nichnai Valley.
  • Pass through Birch and Bhoj trees while crossing Shekdur Meadow.
  • From Shekdur, enjoy a smooth slope lined with Maple Trees.
  • After 2 hours of walking, reach Sonamarg Valley, a well-known tourist spot in Jammu and Kashmir.
  • Drive back to Srinagar for your onward journey.
